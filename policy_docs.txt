DEBT-TO-INCOME RATIO POLICY (Section 3.2)

The debt-to-income (DTI) ratio is a primary underwriting criterion. It
measures the applicant's total monthly debt obligations as a percentage
of gross monthly income.

Thresholds:
  - DTI <= 20%  : Low risk. Eligible for all loan products.
  - DTI 20–35%  : Moderate risk. Standard underwriting applies.
  - DTI 35–43%  : Elevated risk. Senior underwriter review required.
  - DTI > 43%   : High risk. Application must be declined unless
                  exceptional compensating factors are documented.

Compensating factors that may offset a high DTI:
  - Significant liquid assets (>12 months of payments in reserve)
  - Excellent credit history (no delinquencies in 5 years)
  - Low loan-to-value ratio on secured loans
  - Strong income growth trajectory with documentation

INTEREST RATE AND LOAN GRADE POLICY (Section 4.1)

Interest rates are assigned based on the applicant's credit grade (A–G)
and loan term. Higher rates reflect higher perceived credit risk.

Rate bands by grade:
  - Grade A : 5.0% – 8.0%
  - Grade B : 8.0% – 11.0%
  - Grade C : 11.0% – 14.0%
  - Grade D : 14.0% – 18.0%
  - Grade E : 18.0% – 22.0%
  - Grade F : 22.0% – 26.0%
  - Grade G : 26.0%+

Applications with interest rates above 20% require enhanced income
verification. Rates above 25% require documentation of the applicant's
awareness of total repayment costs.

CREDIT UTILISATION POLICY (Section 5.3)

Revolving credit utilisation reflects how much of an applicant's
available credit is currently in use.

Guidelines:
  - Utilisation <= 30%  : Acceptable. No additional review required.
  - Utilisation 30–60%  : Moderate concern. Consider alongside DTI.
  - Utilisation 60–80%  : High concern. Request explanation from applicant.
  - Utilisation > 80%   : Very high. Strong negative signal. Decline
                          unless strong compensating factors present.

Note: Sudden increases in utilisation (>20pp in 3 months) may indicate
financial distress and warrant additional investigation.

LOAN-TO-INCOME RATIO POLICY (Section 3.4)

The loan-to-income (LTI) ratio measures the requested loan amount
relative to the applicant's annual gross income.

Thresholds:
  - LTI <= 0.20 : Low risk. Standard processing.
  - LTI 0.20–0.35: Moderate. Standard underwriting applies.
  - LTI 0.35–0.50: Elevated. Verify income documentation.
  - LTI > 0.50  : High. Senior review required. Decline if DTI also elevated.

DEROGATORY MARKS POLICY (Section 6.1)

Derogatory marks include public records (bankruptcies, judgements, liens)
and recent delinquencies (missed payments within the past 24 months).

Policy:
  - 0 derogatory marks      : No impact on processing.
  - 1 derogatory mark       : Document the circumstances. Review required
                              if mark is within 12 months.
  - 2+ derogatory marks     : Senior underwriter review required.
  - Bankruptcy within 2 yrs : Automatic decline.
  - Bankruptcy 2–7 yrs ago  : Eligible with strong compensating factors
                              and documented recovery.

INCOME VERIFICATION POLICY (Section 2.1)

All loan applications must include income verification at one of three levels:
  - Not Verified   : Stated income only. Applies to loans < $10,000
                     with Grade A–B applicants.
  - Source Verified: Income source confirmed (employer, bank). Required
                     for loans $10,000–$25,000 or Grade C–D.
  - Verified       : Full income documentation (tax returns, pay stubs).
                     Required for loans > $25,000 or Grade E–G.

Applications with income verification below the required level for their
loan amount and grade must be escalated before proceeding.

CREDIT GRADE POLICY (Section 4.2)

Credit grades (A–G) are assigned based on the applicant's FICO score,
credit history length, and payment behaviour.

Grade descriptions:
  - Grade A : Excellent credit. FICO 750+. No delinquencies.
  - Grade B : Good credit. FICO 700–749. Minor issues acceptable.
  - Grade C : Fair credit. FICO 650–699. Some delinquencies permitted.
  - Grade D : Below average. FICO 600–649. Enhanced review required.
  - Grade E : Poor credit. FICO 550–599. Approval requires strong income.
  - Grade F : Very poor. FICO 500–549. Decline in most circumstances.
  - Grade G : Exceptional risk. FICO < 500. Decline.

GENERAL LENDING POLICY (Section 1.1)

All lending decisions must be made in accordance with applicable fair
lending laws, including the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and
the Fair Housing Act. Decisions must be based solely on creditworthiness
criteria and not on protected characteristics.

The machine learning model score is one input into the underwriting
decision. Loan officers retain discretion to:
  - Approve a borderline application with documented compensating factors
  - Decline an application that scores as low-risk if fraud is suspected
  - Escalate any application to a senior underwriter

All overrides must be documented in the loan management system with a
clear rationale. Systematic overrides in one direction may trigger a
fair lending review.
